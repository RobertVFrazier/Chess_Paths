Information

Welcome to Chess Paths, a simple puzzle game.


In the title screen, just click on the queen to start the puzzle. This will take you to the main game board. The buttons there will give you everything you need to play the game.


NOTE: When you are on the game board screen and on the Save and Load Games screen, the program will be in full-screen mode. You can get back to normal screen mode by returing to the splash screen.

Rules

Instructions for playing the Chess Paths queen puzzle.


Here is the challenge as originally written by Henry Ernest Dudeney: 'To cover all the cells of the chessboard going from the cell c5 to the cell d4 in exactly 14 legal movements of the queen.'



(By the way, there are other paths for the queen to visit all of the squares in 14 moves, but not ending up one square away from where she started. And there are many more ways to cover the board in 15 moves.)


Click or tap on the chessboard to place the queen. Placing the queen does not count as a move, but it does mark the square as visited.


You have fourteen moves to visit each square on the chessboard, starting from C5 and ending on D4, one square away from where you started. A square is visited if the queen has either moved through it or landed on it.


So, if you were to start on square A1, lower left corner, and move to square H1, lower right corner, that is one move, with eight squares visited.


Only moves which are legal for a chess queen to make will be allowed. If you are not familiar with the legal moves for a queen, click the Moves button below.



Use the Undo and Redo buttons (the curving left and right arrow buttons) as often as you like. There is no penalty in the game for undoing moves. The Reset button will reset the game to before you placed the queen on the board.


Click the Load/Save button to open a new screen. You will need to sign up (or log in, if you have already signed up) to save or load games. You will only need to give a user name and password. It's only to keep different people's games separate. I won't collect any other information from you.


That's it. Have fun, and good luck!



Moves

How to move the queen on a chess board.


In short, a queen can move in straight lines only: horizontally, vertically, or in 45 degree diagonals, any number of squares, until she reaches the edge of the board.


So, where can't a queen move? Any diagonal that is not exactly 45 degrees.


In modern chess notation, the squares are referred to with two characters, a letter followed by a number. The letter refers to the column, A to H, left to right. The number refers to the row, 1 to 8, bottom to top.


(Note that the lower right corner, H1, is always a white square. If you are setting up a chess game in real life and H1 is black, rotate the board 90 degrees. In an actual game, the white pieces are on the bottom two rows and the black pieces are on the top two rows.)


In the diagram below, the queen is on E5. From E5, she can move to any of the squares with the green target symbol. All of the other squares would be illegal moves from this position.
